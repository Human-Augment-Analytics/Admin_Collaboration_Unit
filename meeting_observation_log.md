## Meeting Observations

### Image Processing Unit — 2026-02-27
- **Host/Facilitator:** Dr. Mussmann
- **Attendees (approx):** 9
- **Duration:** ~1 hr 19 min (originally scheduled for 1 hr; last ~20 min was unit manager + faculty discussing next steps)
- **Meeting Structure:** Yes — two clear halves. First half: Dr. Mussmann presented a topic (Weak Supervision). Second half: one project group shared their update. Post-meeting: unit manager stayed with faculty to discuss logistics and next steps.
- **Discussion Quality (1-5):** 4
- **Cross-Project Discussion:** Yes — the topic presentation (Weak Supervision) was broadly relevant across projects. Covered incomplete supervision, noisy supervision, and semi-supervised learning with examples from medical imaging, remote sensing, NLP, and more. Multiple projects could relate their own work to the concepts discussed.
- **Researcher Contributions:** One project group presented their update in the second half
- **Advisor/Faculty Role:** Dr. Mussmann led the first half with a structured 19-slide presentation. Stayed after to discuss next steps with the unit manager.
- **Key Topics Discussed:** Weak supervision methods including aggregated labels, censored labels, labeling rules, external systems, auxiliary signals, crowdsourced labels, self-training, representation learning, consistency-based methods, and co-training/multi-view learning.
- **Problems Raised:** Not documented
- **What Worked:** Faculty-led topic presentation gave the meeting real substance and educational value. The topic was chosen to be relevant across multiple projects. Having a clear two-part structure (topic + project update) kept the meeting organized. High attendance (9 people) compared to other units.
- **What Didn't Work:** Meeting ran 19 minutes over scheduled time. The last 20 minutes was only unit manager + faculty, not the full group — unclear if this should be a separate meeting.

## Notes & Emerging Patterns

- **Facilitator presence is the single biggest factor.** Dr. Mussmann's Image Processing meeting had 9 attendees and ran 1hr 19min with active discussion. UI and 3-D Vision meetings without a faculty facilitator had 2 and 1 attendees respectively, lasting 11-15 minutes.
- **Structure matters.** The Image Processing meeting had a clear two-part format (topic presentation + project update) that kept things moving. The other two meetings had no agenda at all.
- **Faculty-led topic presentations create cross-project value.** Dr. Mussmann's Weak Supervision talk was relevant to multiple projects, giving researchers a shared reference point. This is exactly the kind of knowledge transfer Bree intended.
- Meetings without a facilitator or agenda collapse almost immediately (11 min and 15 min)
- No evidence of any pre-meeting reminders or accountability for attendance in the cold meetings
- Open question: should the unit manager + faculty post-meeting discussion (last 20 min of Image Processing) be formalized as a separate recurring check-in?
